Dio



    

The Honda Dio is a motor scooter made by Honda from 1988–1996 and Honda Motorcycle and Scooter India (HMSI) since 1996. It was introduced in Japan in January 1988. The name is derived from the son of Venus "dyonisiac"[clarify]. It is now manufactured and assembled in India by Honda's HMSI subsidiary, from where it is exported to UK as the Honda Lead. The Dio is equipped with cushioned seats and an under-seat storage box up to 18 litres.

Model history and specifications

The Japanese Dio was equipped with the two-stroke 50 cc (3.1 cu in) AF18E single-cylinder engine with electric start. It is superficially similar to the USA-only Honda "Elite S”. The Japanese Honda Dio was sold in 17 different models, and used the model code “af” with numbers from 18~35 e.g.: af28, it also has huge aftermarket support therefore, has been widely known in the Asian scooter scene. Later versions in some markets were equipped with a 102 cc (6.2 cu in) air-cooled, four-stroke, single-cylinder, OHC engine with both electric self-start and kick start. This model was not exported to the USA. The Indian version has a 6-litre (1.3 imp gal; 1.6 US gal) fuel tank, of which one litre is reserve, and the Japanese version has a 4-litre (0.88 imp gal; 1.1 US gal) fuel tank with no reserve. In 2012, HMSI launched a new Dio at Auto Expo in India. It has a new 110 cc (6.7 cu in) engine, which also runs Honda's Activa and Aviator. It also has a new look and new headlight. The 2013 HMSI Dio had claimed improvements in fuel efficiency and combined brakes. In 2017, Honda launched the revamped version of the Dio, complying with Bharat Standard - IV (BS-IV) Emissions regulations, featuring a redesigned speedometer design, along with the inclusion of an LED headlight. Later in 2018, Honda launched the Dio DX, featuring digital speedometer and power seats, while retaining the look of the 2017 model.

Honda Dio Overview

Honda brings in new body graphics and fresh styling for the Dio. It is one of the top selling scooters by Honda which got well appreciated by young crowds looking for sporty design, frugal engine and comfort. With much design changes and squeezing the most efficiency from this reliable urban scooter, Honda is set to aim at higher sales targets this time around. It features a full LED headlight on the front of the scooter enhancing its beefy and bold appeal. Indicators have been integrated to the headlights as earlier adding a wilder look to its personality. New colours have dual-tone colours which looks excellent along with the gold finished steel rims which look probably the best in segment.

Honda Dio Performance and Handling

Honda Dio Deluxe has excellent handling and ride comfort. To ensure stronger braking, Dio comes with 130mm drum brakes both front and rear, along with Honda’s CBS braking for safety. Suspension is integrated from the bottom of the scooter via hydraulic damper spring suspension system. This next generation Dio scooty achieves a top speed of 83kmph, making it a good enough for longer distances too. Dimensions are very compact that encourage smooth and superior riding quality in traffic congested roads. Creating a huge buzz with its innovation Honda Dio upgraded model will garner a lot of attention among the younger generation fighting a major battle with TVS Wego,Mahindra Gusto,Hero Pleasure,Suzuki Lets and Yamaha Ray in the segment.

Honda Dio Engine Specifications & Transmission

The 109.2cc, single cylinder, air-cooled engine powering Dio is good for maximum power of 8bhp at 7000rpm along with peak torque of 8.91Nm at 5500rpm.Mated to a V-matic transmission, Dio is claimed to offer top-speed of 83km/h. Moreover, the HET technology equipped engine is claimed to deliver impressive mileage.

Honda Dio Features

Features available on Dio include a new 3D emblem, sporty body graphics, 18 litres of under storage space, wide seat, comfortable leg space in front, combi-brake system and tubeless tyres among others.

Honda Dio Braking & Handling

Braking force is derived from 130mm drum units in front and rear. ABS is missing in the model, but it features combi-brake system which brings front and rear brakes into action at the same time when rear brake lever is pulled.

Honda Dio Wheels & Tyres

Honda has used 90/100 sized tubeless tyres in front and back and the rim size is similar on both ends measuring around 10 inch.

Honda Dio Competition

The 110cc scooter faces stiff competition from a number of models including Yamaha Alpha, Mahindra Gusto, Hero Maestro and TVS Scooty Zest 110 to name a few.
